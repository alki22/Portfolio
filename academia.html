Academic Experience

Since 2018, I have participated in multiple academic projects on NLP-related topics:
  • Member of the project AGGREEY
    • Coordinator: Srdjan Vesic
    • Start: January 2023
    • End: April 2026
    • Topics: Explainable Machine Learning-based Artificial Intelligence (XAI), E-Democracy, Argumentation, Logics
    • Funded by French National Research Agency (ANR): ANR grant ANR-22-CE23-0005
    • As part of this project, I made the following contributions:
      • Participated in the organizing comité of the École d'automne IA2: INTELLIGENCE ARTIFICIELLE et DÉMOCRATIE
      • Conducting experiments to improve the state-of-the-art on the sentence text similarity (STS) task.
      • Annotated datasets with AMR graphs features and linguistic aspects using LLMs and deterministic algorithms.
      • Right now, I'm using text translation into sets of First Order Logic clauses in order to improve the results on STS.
    • Member of the project ANTIDOTE - "ArgumeNtaTIon-Driven explainable artificial intelligence fOr digiTal mEdicine"
      • Coordinators: Elena Cabrio and Serena Villata
      • Start: January 2021
      • End: July 2024
      • Topics: Explainable Machine Learning-based Artificial Intelligence (XAI), Digital Medicine
      • Funded by chist-era
      • As part of this project, I made the following contributions:
        • Actively participated of the ACTA pipeline and its demo development
        • Also, I participated of the ANTIDOTE project's demo development and maintenance. Plus, I co-authored the corresponding demo paper presented at ECAI 2024
        • Finally, I co-authored the paper On the Automatic Assessment of Natural Language Expert Explanations in Medicine and participated in the experimentation and development of the pipeline
      • Member of the project "Procesamiento automático de diálogo para mejorar el éxito en tareas" :
        Backed and funded by Secretaría de Ciencia y Técnica de la Universidad Nacional de Córdoba. Resolution SeCyT-UNC 411/18.
        • Director: Luciana Benotti
        • Start: 2018
        • End: 2022
        • As part of this project, I completed my masters thesis "Predicción de tiempos de respuesta de estudiantes en diálogos"

      Before starting my academic path, I've been working as a data scientist for two years and finished my licenciatura (5-year masters equivalent in Argentina) on Computer Science. To achieve the later, I've completed my thesis "Students response times prediction in dialogue" (Predicción de tiempos de respuesta de estudiantes en diálogos) under the supervision of Luciana Benotti. This work combined applications of Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Natural Language & Dialogue Processing

      Additionally, some other academic activities include:

      • Mentor at ‘Diplomatura en ciencia de datos, aprendizaje automático y sus aplicaciones’, 2020, FaMAF, UNC. This mentorship involved to determine how can we classify songs into different genres according to what do their data tell us about them. As part of the mentorship's roadmap, we conducted many Data Analyses and developed many Deep Learning models, finishing with presentations made by each of the mentorees.
      • UNC++: assisted teachers in helping high school students to learn Arduino programming to promote the population's interest in computer science.
      • Attendee and part of the volunteer staff in PyData Córdoba 2019.
      • Attendee and part of the volunteer staff in PyData Córdoba 2018.
      • Attendee at ACM-ICPC Training Camp in 2014.

      I've also completed many post-graduate-level courses along the way:

      • Mathematical models in quantitative finance. Prof. Patricia Kisbye. 2C 2018. FaMAF-UNC.
      • Automatic dialogue processing. Prof. Luciana Benotti. 1C 2019 FaMAF-UNC.
